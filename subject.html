About Badminton

Badminton is a indoor sport played with a racket and a shuttlecock. The sport dates back to Ancient Greece, China, and India. It is played on a court with the dimensions of 44 feet by 17 feet and a 5 feet net. The goal is to hit the shuttlecock onto the desinated area on the other side of the net. If the opponent returns the shuttlecock over net; it is called a rally and continues until one messes up. Britannica; Badminton

Rules of Badminton

The players arent allowed to touch the net with both their bodies and racket. The shuttlecock shouldn't go outside of the boundary lines. The birdie shouldn't hit the ceiling or rest on a racket. Players can't move until the shuttlecock is served. To serve the birdie, you stand on the service line and the racket should be under the waist. Serve with an underhand motion. Rules of Badminton

Scoring and Gameplay

The game starts off with an coin flip. Whoever calls the right side gets to serve first. The two teams play a total of three games and have to reach a score of 21 inorder to win. If the score is tied 20-20, the first team to get ahead two points win. If it continues to tie, the first to 30 wins. Each team switch sides between every game. In order to win, you need to win 2 out of 3 three games. Game of Badminton
